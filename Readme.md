## 🚀 The 5 Sharpest Product Feedbacks

### 1. GTM & ICP: The Stranger Trust Gap in Public Groups
* **(a) Observed:** The app allows users to join "Public Groups" to split costs for digital subscriptions.
* **(b) Problem:** **The Trust Deficit.** If a stranger hosting the group defaults or changes the login credentials, other members lose their money. This causes high user churn.
* **(c) Ship Instead:** **Smart Escrow & Subspace-Led Billing.** Instead of peer-to-peer transfers, Subspace should collect funds securely and pay the vendor directly.

### 2. Feature: Hyper-Local Subscription Discovery
* **(a) Observed:** Subspace is positioned as India's first subscription marketplace for local providers.
* **(b) Problem:** **Discovery Cold Start.** While digital gift cards and major streaming services are visible[cite: 1], local utility/gym/internet providers are buried or non-existent depending on the user's geo-location.
* **(c) Ship Instead:** **Location-Based "Near Me" Subscription Feeds.** Use device GPS to surface hyper-local internet service providers (ISPs), local gym chains, or regional newspaper subscriptions directly on the home feed.

### 3. UX: Subscription Auto-Detection Friction
* **(a) Observed:** The platform uses APIs to auto-detect and categorize recurring payments[cite: 1].
* **(b) Problem:** **The Hidden Long-Tail.** While major platforms are caught easily, smaller recurring local charges or SaaS add-ons are missed, forcing manual entry which increases user drop-off.
* **(c) Ship Instead:** **SMS/Email Parsing Consent Flow.** Implement an opt-in secure SMS/Email reader agent during onboarding to parse invoice alerts, completely eliminating manual tracking logs.

### 4. Competitor Analysis: Network Effects vs. Churn
* **(a) Observed:** The platform relies heavily on group sharing network effects[cite: 1].
* **(b) Problem:** Entertainment subscriptions are highly seasonal (users cancel when a show ends). This transactional mindset hurts Subspace's customer lifetime value (LTV).
* **(c) Ship Instead:** **Private Group Utility Bundling.** Incentivize users to create private groups with real friends for high-retention, essential bills (e.g., Wi-Fi, electricity, house rent sharing) by offering milestone discount rewards.

### 5. Potential Collaborations: The Student Ecosystem
* **(a) Observed:** The core user base consists of cost-conscious consumers utilizing bill splitting and group finance[cite: 1].
* **(b) Problem:** Acquiring these users individually via performance marketing eats into bootstrapping margins.
* **(c) Ship Instead:** **Co-Branded University Bundles.** Partner with student housing networks (like Stanza Living, Zolostays) or ed-tech platforms. Pre-integrate Subspace as the default utility/room-expense manager for student cohorts moving into new apartments.
